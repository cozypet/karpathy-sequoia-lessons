Karpathy at Sequoia: 5 architectural lessons
What He Said vs. What the Market Heard vs. What to Build
Twelve recap articles missed the design decisions hiding in plain sight. Here are all five at once.
Lesson
Karpathy said
Market heard
What to build
1
Jaggedness
Coverage map, not a bug
Models spike inside RL training distributions and fail outside them. "Walk to the car wash 50m away" still breaks Opus 4.7.
"Models are imperfect. Stay in the loop. Treat them as tools."
Map RL circuits for your domain. Route high-autonomy tasks inside, heavy verification outside.
2
Verification
Architecture, not prompting
"What you can verify, you can automate." Generation-verification loop speed is the lever.
"Add output checks. Use LLM judges. Implement a review step."
Build automated test harnesses and behavioral contracts. If a human reads the output, you didn't automate.
3
Agent-native infra
Legible, not accessible
"Why are people still telling me what to do? What's the thing I should copy-paste to my agent?"
"Rewrite your docs for agents. Make them copy-pasteable."
Redesign system surfaces: typed APIs, structured errors, idempotent ops, explicit state.
4
Taste
Constraints, not hiring
The Stripe/Google email bug: agent matched users by email because nothing told it to use persistent IDs.
"Hire people with good judgment. Stay in the loop."
Encode taste into schema rules and behavioral tests. Constraints survive vacations; judgment doesn't.
5
Understanding
The moat, not the model
"You can outsource your thinking but you can't outsource your understanding."
"Stay curious. Keep learning. Humans still matter."
Invest learning budget in domain depth, not model access. Domain depth compounds; model access commoditizes.
Karpathy gave us the diagnosis. The treatment is architecture.
